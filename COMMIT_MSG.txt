dequote: join records split by line breaks inside quoted fields

The previous change (dropping blank lines) was the wrong cure. The real defect is a record split
across physical lines because a quoted field contains a real line break, e.g. a NOTE field whose
text starts with a quote and continues on the next line. The executor read ONE PHYSICAL LINE at a
time (readLine + parseCsv), so such a record was never reassembled: the parser saw a fragment with
an unterminated quote and wrote it out still split.

Records are now read as LOGICAL rows: readCsvRecord() keeps appending physical lines while the
double quotes on the record are unbalanced (oddQuotes; RFC "" escapes keep the parity even so they
never trigger a join), replacing the embedded break. Guards: at most 5000 continuation lines per
record, and an unterminated quote at EOF returns what has been read rather than hanging or
swallowing the rest of the file. Header and body are read the same way, and field values are
sanitised so no CR/LF can survive inside a written field.

New step parameter `embeddedNewlines` (designer dropdown "Line breaks inside quoted fields"):
space (default, the break becomes a single space), strip (the break is removed) or keep (legacy,
the record stays split). New output variable `embeddedNewlinesRemoved` counting the physical lines
joined, reported in the summary log next to blankLinesRemoved; the executor description was
updated accordingly.

Docs: .claude/2026-07-23-dequote-embedded-newlines.md and CLAUDE.md.

Verify: oddQuotes and readCsvRecord were extracted from the source, compiled and executed -- the
split record collapses into a single record in space and strip mode, stays split in keep mode,
escaped "" do not cause a join, and an unterminated quote at EOF terminates cleanly. designer.html
passes node --check with no literal \n/\r and no unsafe Thymeleaf; InternalSteps is brace-balanced.
