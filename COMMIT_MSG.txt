feat(loop): live run-time animation - iteration label + xN badges

At run time the diagram now animates the loop:
- an "iteration N / total" label appears near the LOOP node;
- each body block shows a "xN" badge (current pass);
- the ENDLOOP -> LOOP back-arrow pulses and the body blocks flash when
  the pass advances, making the jump-back and re-run visually clear.

Implementation (UI only; no backend change):
- bpmn.js: a loops registry is built while drawing the back-edges
  (edge + body node ids, each body node mapped to its innermost loop).
  New setLoopState(loopId, iter, count) draws/updates the label and the
  per-block badges, pulses the edge and flashes the bodies on advance.
  addCls/rmCls toggle classes without clobbering polled status classes.
- run.html: LOOP_IDS captured from the definition; the poll reads
  __loop.<id>.i and .n from run.vars (already in the status response)
  and calls setLoopState (iter = i + 1, count = n), clearing when the
  keys disappear. Internal __ vars are now hidden from the vars dump.

bpmn.js and run.html validated; no \n/\r; Thymeleaf-safe. Verify on UBS
with a multi-file loop (very fast steps may advance between 2s polls).
